Hinduism is the oldest religion in the world that is still widely-practiced today, having been formed over 4000 years ago. Arguably the world’s first monotheistic faith, it’s one of the oldest religions still in existence. Its practice has historically been most prominent in East and Southeast Asia.
Hindus most commonly live in India, Nepal, Bangladesh, Indonesia, Pakistan, Sri Lanka, and Malaysia.
Five elements shape Hindu religious tradition: doctrine, practice, society, story, and devotion. The Veda is ancient Hindu religious literature that is the basis of later holy texts as well. Hindus also accept and believe in the ideas of karma, samsara, and moksha.
Every year around October and November, Hindus around the world celebrate Diwali, or Deepavali—a festival of lights that stretches back more than 2,500 years.
