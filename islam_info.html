Islam is a monotheistic religion that was formed in the seventh century, and it is the world’s second-largest religion and the fastest-growing. The religion was formed by and is based on the teachings of the Prophet Muhammad. The main regions of the world that practice Islam are in Central Asia, the Middle East, Western Asia, and some of Southeast Asia. A few examples of these countries are Saudi Arabia, Pakistan, Iran, Algeria, and Morocco.
Islam is mostly made up of two denominations: Sunni and Shia. There are also five pillars of Islam that Muslims adhere to: Shahadah, Salat, Zakat, Sawm, and Hajj. Shahadah is a sincere recitation of the Muslim profession of faither. Salat is the act of performing prayers properly five times a day. Zakat is donating 2.5% or more of your wealth to the poor. Sawm is a fast during Ramadan. Hajj is a pilgrimage to Mecca.
